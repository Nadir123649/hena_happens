What is Henna?

Henna is a term used for a paste made from the leaves of the hennaplant, or Lawsonia inermis.

Our recipe is simply the ground henna leafpowder mixed with lemon juice, essential oils (cajeput, lavender, eucalyptus, tea tree), and sugar.

Ancient uses of henna include anti-fungal properties for medical use, skin, hair, leather, and fabric dye, beautification, and ceremonial use, many of which are still in practicetoday.

Henna is not recommended for those with G6PD, a hereditarydeficiency.

Please inform your artist if you are pregnant. We offer maternal henna, our lavender essential oil only blend. This blend is the most gentle andalso recommended for chemotherapy patients or those with sensitive skin.

PSA: Black “henna” warning: Without paying attention it can be easy toget natural henna mixed up with the chemical slurry that’s called “black henna”. Please avoid the artificial shelf stable cones, they canbe full of toxic preservatives, hair dye (PPD), and other ingredients thatcould potentially cause toxic poisoning or chemical burns.

How to be safe: Ask your henna artist exactly what the ingredient listis, including essential oils, and what color the stain will be. Naturalhenna starts as orange and takes 2-3 days to oxidizae into a red browncolor.

Want the tattoo look? Meet Jagua!

Jagua is a fruit, Genipa Americana, from South America which stainsthe skin a navy black color. Native groups have been using this fruit forbeautification and medicinal use.

Jagua, a distant relative to the kiwi, is picked prematurely and juiced. With water consistency, it is then thickened to a gel with food gradexanthan gum and a touch of lavender essential oil to make our jaguagel.

Allergy notice: Although jagua is a fruit based dye, those with kiwiallergies or skin conditions such as psoriasis, eczema, or generallysensitive skin may get a reaction or flare up due to its acidity

Aftercare: Both henna and jagua

The paste or gel will take ~1 hour to dry and set, during this time becareful to avoid contact of any kind to prevent smearing. Even afterfixing, the smear may still appear.

Both stains darkest towards the hands and feet and lightest towardsthe center of the body, depending on the thickness of your skin.

Designs take 2 days to darken and typically last 1-2 weeks dependingon lifestyle, skin type, and aftercare.

Henna Aftercare

Once the paste has dried do not pick off your design. Leave it on for 4-6 hours minimum, longer the better. Henna will crack and flake off onits own, this is normal and will reveal a bright orange color.

Avoidwetting the area for the first 12 hours and remove any left over pastewith oil. The orange stain will oxidize and darken to a red brown colorover the next 2-3 days.

Moisturize regularly to extend the life of your design. Avoid exfoliation, harsh soaps, hot tubs, and pools, these will all shorten the life of yourdesign.

Jagua Aftercare

Once the gel has set, avoid friction directly over the design as it maytransfer or shift. Leave it on for 2-3 hours minimum, longer the better.

Do not sleep or swim with your design.

Before sleeping or swimming, use soap and water, lather and wash thegel off thoroughly. Once squeaky clean, give the area and surroundingarea, wherever water dripped down and the hand you used to wash it afinal rinse to make sure no shadows appear.

The design will be invisibleat first but will slowly oxidize and darken over the next 24-48 hours.
